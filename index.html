I am an undergraduate student at Seoul National University, double majoring in Mechanical Engineering and Electrical and Computer Engineering. I am interested in hardware-algorithm co-design for real-time embedded physical systems, especially FPGA/RTL acceleration for robotics, vision, control, and power-constrained on-device computing. My recent experience includes FPGA-based secure camera pipeline development at Mutual.ai and actuation modeling for a large aerial robot system at SNU INRoL.

Current Interests

I am exploring how embedded hardware can better support physical systems with strict latency, power, and reliability constraints. I am especially interested in designing power-efficient RTL/HLS accelerators that make on-device perception, inference, and control more practical for robots and drones.

What kind of hardware-algorithm co-design is needed to run on-device inference, vision processing, and control algorithms within limited power and latency budgets?

Technical Focus

  • FPGA/RTL design for real-time embedded systems.
  • Hardware-algorithm co-design for robotics, vision, and control workloads.
  • Power-efficient on-device processing under latency and reliability constraints.
  • Hardware/software integration for physical AI systems.
